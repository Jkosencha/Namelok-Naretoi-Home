Vivian's Profile

  • Name: Vivian
  • Age: 13
  • Gender: Female
  • Status: Rescued from early marriage

On the evening of December 12, 2023, at approximately 7:30 pm, a 15-year-old Maasai girl sought refuge at Namelok Naretoi Home, visibly distressed. Extending a warm welcome, I received her, and once she had composed herself, she shared her poignant narrative. This young girl, an orphan, lost her mother during her early childhood, and her father, facing mental health challenges, left home. Subsequently, she and three brothers were taken in by one of their uncles, who assumed responsibility for their care. Enrolled in a local primary school near her home, she diligently pursued her studies, culminating in her final exams in October 2023, with aspirations to join high school. Upon receiving the exam results, her joy at securing admission to secondary school swiftly transformed into anguish. Disturbingly, she learned that her uncle, in exchange for numerous cattle, intended to subject her to female genital mutilation (FGM) and subsequently marry her off to an elderly affluent man. This grim revelation left the girl grappling with thoughts of suicide, torn between the oppressive cultural norms and her desperate need for escape. Recalling an organization called Namelok Naretoi, which she had encountered during an awareness program at her local school, she made the courageous decision to flee, ultimately finding refuge at Namelok. Recognizing the severity of the situation, Namelok Naretoi decided to provide a scholarship for the girl, ensuring her continued education. The organization reported the case to the local chief and government officer, who expressed gratitude to Namelok for its support and pledged protection in the face of any potential threats. The girl, now filled with gratitude, envisions a brighter future, promising to complete her education and return to serve her community.
